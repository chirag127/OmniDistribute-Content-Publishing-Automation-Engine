## Understanding Credit Bureaus in India

India has **four major credit bureaus** licensed by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI):

1.  **TransUnion CIBIL** - India's oldest and most widely used credit bureau (established in 2000)
2.  **Experian** - Global credit information company operating in India since 2010
3.  **Equifax** - Operating in India since 2010 as a joint venture
4.  **CRIF High Mark** - Specializes in retail, microfinance, MSMEs, and commercial borrowers

Each bureau uses its own algorithm to calculate credit scores, which is why your score may vary slightly across different bureaus. However, **CIBIL remains the most preferred** by over 90% of Indian lenders.

## Understanding Credit Score Ranges

### CIBIL/Experian/Equifax/CRIF Score Ranges:

| Score Range | Rating    | Loan Approval Chances      |
| ----------- | --------- | -------------------------- |
| **300-549** | Poor      | Very Difficult             |
| **550-649** | Average   | Difficult, higher interest |
| **650-699** | Fair      | Moderate                   |
| **700-749** | Good      | Good chances               |
| **750-900** | Excellent | Very High, best rates      |

### What Affects Your Credit Score?

1.  **Payment History (35%)** - Most important factor

    -   Pay all EMIs and credit card bills on time
    -   Even one missed payment impacts your score significantly

2.  **Credit Utilization (30%)** - Keep below 30%

    -   Don't max out your credit cards
    -   Maintain low Credit Utilization Ratio (CUR)

3.  **Credit History Length (15%)**

    -   Longer credit history is better
    -   Don't close old credit card accounts

4.  **Credit Mix (10%)**

    -   Healthy mix of secured and unsecured loans
    -   Credit cards + loans show diverse credit management

5.  **Credit Inquiries (10%)**
    -   Too many loan applications hurt your score
    -   Hard inquiries lower your score temporarily

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does checking my credit score affect it?**
A: No! Checking your own score is a "soft inquiry" and doesn't impact your credit score.

**Q: Why are my credit scores different across bureaus?**
A: Each bureau uses different algorithms and may receive data at different times from lenders.

**Q: Which credit score do banks prefer?**
A: In India, over 90% of lenders prefer CIBIL scores, but many also check Experian.

**Q: Can I check all 4 bureau scores for free?**
A: Yes! PaisaBazaar is the only platform offering all 4 bureau scores for free.

**Q: How often should I check my credit score?**
A: At least once a month, especially if you're planning to apply for loans or credit cards.

**Q: What's the minimum score needed for loan approval?**
A: Most lenders prefer 750+, but some NBFCs may approve loans with scores as low as 650-700.

**Q: Can I improve my credit score quickly?**
A: It takes 3-6 months of consistent good credit behavior to see significant improvements.

**Q: Is WhatsApp credit score checking safe?**

## How to Improve Your Credit Score

1.  **Pay all bills on time** - Set up auto-debit for EMIs and credit card payments
2.  **Keep credit utilization below 30%** - Don't use more than 30% of your credit limit
3.  **Don't apply for too much credit** - Space out loan/credit card applications
4.  **Maintain old credit accounts** - Don't close old credit cards
5.  **Check for errors regularly** - Dispute inaccuracies immediately
6.  **Diversify your credit** - Mix of credit cards and loans
7.  **Pay off outstanding debts** - Clear any pending dues
8.  **Avoid settling debts** - Always pay in full, not "settled"
